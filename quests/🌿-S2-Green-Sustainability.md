## 📖 Theory Bite

Sustainability science is about **understanding Earth systems and finding leverage points for change**.

| 🧩 Concept | 💡 What it means |
|---------|---------------|
| 🌐 Planetary boundaries | 9 Earth-system processes with quantified limits (climate, biodiversity, freshwater...) |
| 🏭 Carbon accounting | Scope 1 (direct), Scope 2 (energy), Scope 3 (supply chain) emissions |
| ♻️ Life cycle assessment (LCA) | Total environmental impact from cradle to grave |
| 🔄 Systems thinking | Interventions ripple — biofuels reduce carbon but increase land use |

> 💼 **Daily reality:** Data analysis, policy writing, stakeholder negotiation, and managing the tension between scientific urgency and political/economic reality.

**⏱ Time:** ~45 min | **Difficulty:** ⭐ Beginner | **Unlock:** S2.2

**Quest:** Map one sustainability problem into a structured analysis.

**Steps:**
1. 🎯 Pick one climate or sustainability problem
2. 🗺️ Fill in the map:

```
🌍 SUSTAINABILITY MAP
├── 🔥 Problem: What's happening?
├── 👥 Actors: Who causes it? Who is affected? Who could change it?
├── 🔧 Interventions: What could work?
│   ├── 🟢 High-impact, low-cost
│   ├── 🟡 High-impact, high-cost
│   └── 🔴 Low-impact, popular (greenwashing alert!)
```

### 🗝️ Quest S2.5 — Policy Brief
**⏱ Time:** ~60 min | **Difficulty:** ⭐⭐⭐ Advanced | **Unlock:** 🏆 Quest Complete!

**Quest:** Write a 1-page policy brief recommending one sustainability action to a local government.

**Steps:**
1. 📋 Structure your brief:

```
🏛️ POLICY BRIEF
├── 📊 The Problem (2-3 sentences with data)
├── 🎯 Recommended Action (be specific)
├── 📈 Expected Impact (quantify if possible)
├── 💰 Cost Estimate (rough is fine)
├── ⚠️ Main Objection + Your Rebuttal
└── 📅 Timeline for Implementation
```

2. 📚 Use at least 2 data sources (cite them)
3. ✂️ Keep it under 500 words — policymakers don't read long documents

> 💭 **Reflection:** Could you handle writing for decision-makers who may not share your urgency?
